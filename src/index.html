Edgar Martínez in the Hall of Fame

Edgar Martínez is headed for Cooperstown. The graceful, mild-mannered slugger became perhaps the greatest designated hitter to ever play the game over his storied 18-year career in Seattle, and now, his career will be forever remembered in baseball's Hall of Fame.

Comparison between Martínez in 1992 and 2004

No. 11, known simply as 'Gar by many, was by no means the flashy superstar his Hall-of-Fame teammate Ken Griffey Jr. was. Instead, Edgar was a mark of consistency. He was a steady force in the middle of the M's lineup, and was known for spraying the baseball all over the yard, hitting doubles and driving in runs. His most memorable moment fittingly came on a hit known now as 'The Double' that clinched the ALDS for the Mariners and capped the most iconic moment in team history.

Martínez vs the League

Edgar Martínez, perennially underrated? He was regarded as too old to be a prospect. A torn hamstring relegated him to a career as a designated hitter, overlooked for MVPs and underappreciated by Hall of Fame voters for his nine-year life on the ballot. His reputation will forever be his consistency — and those doubles — but at his peak, Martínez was undoubtedly among the league’s elite.
